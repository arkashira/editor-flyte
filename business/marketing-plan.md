# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
**Primary Positioning Statement:**  
"Editor Flyte: The lightweight, high-performance code editor designed for developers who demand speed and reliability without the bloat."

**Alternative Positioning Statements:**  
1. "Editor Flyte: Code faster and more reliably with a streamlined editor that eliminates the frustrations of traditional IDEs."
2. "Editor Flyte: Your no-nonsense code editor that prioritizes performance, so you can focus on what matters—coding."
3. "Editor Flyte: Experience the power of simplicity in coding with a high-speed editor that never slows you down."

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
1. **Persona 1: Alex the Agile Developer**  
   - **Daily Job:** Works in a fast-paced startup environment, focusing on rapid feature development and iteration.  
   - **Budget:** $50/month for development tools.  
   
2. **Persona 2: Jamie the Freelance Programmer**  
   - **Daily Job:** Manages multiple client projects, requiring a versatile and efficient coding environment.  
   - **Budget:** $30/month for development tools.  
   
3. **Persona 3: Taylor the DevOps Engineer**  
   - **Daily Job:** Integrates and deploys applications, needing a reliable editor for scripting and configuration.  
   - **Budget:** $70/month for development tools.  

## Channels
1. **Developer Forums and Communities (e.g., Reddit, Stack Overflow)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $15 per acquisition through targeted ads and community engagement.  

2. **Content Marketing (Blogs, Tutorials, Webinars)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $10 per acquisition through organic traffic and lead generation.  

3. **Social Media Advertising (LinkedIn, Twitter)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $20 per acquisition through targeted ads focused on developers.  

## Content Cadence
- **Week 1:** Blog post introducing Editor Flyte and its benefits.  
- **Week 2:** Tutorial video on setting up and using Editor Flyte.  
- **Week 3:** Case study featuring a developer's experience with Editor Flyte.  
- **Week 4:** Webinar on optimizing coding speed with Editor Flyte.  
- **Week 5:** Blog post comparing Editor Flyte with traditional IDEs.  
- **Week 6:** User-generated content campaign encouraging feedback and testimonials.  
- **Week 7:** Social media Q&A session with the development team.  
- **Week 8:** Infographic highlighting performance metrics of Editor Flyte.  
- **Week 9:** Blog post on future updates and features based on user feedback.  
- **Week 10:** Launch countdown campaign across all channels.  
- **Week 11:** Launch announcement and promotional offers.  
- **Week 12:** Post-launch follow-up and user engagement campaign.  

## Launch Milestones
- **D-30:** Finalize product features and begin beta testing with selected users.  
- **D-0:** Official launch of Editor Flyte with promotional offers and marketing push.  
- **D+30:** Evaluate user feedback, implement minor updates, and begin targeted marketing campaigns.  
- **D+90:** Assess overall performance, user engagement, and prepare for additional feature releases based on user demand.  

## Success Metrics
- **Daily Active Users (DAU):** Target 1,000 DAU by D+90.  
- **Monthly Recurring Revenue ($MRR):** Target $10,000 MRR by D+90.
